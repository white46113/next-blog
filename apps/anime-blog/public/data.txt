
Best Anime Like Solo Leveling: OP Protagonists & Unique Leveling Systems (2025)
If you loved Solo Leveling for its OP protagonist, satisfying power progression, and epic battles, you’re in the right place. Below, we’ve curated the best anime that deliver the same thrills—whether it’s leveling up, dominating enemies, or exploring dark fantasy worlds.

1. Anime with OP Protagonists & Leveling Systems
These series perfectly blend the zero-to-hero journey with game-like progression, just like Solo Leveling:
A. The Rising of the Shield Hero

Why it’s similar: Naofumi starts as a shunned hero but levels up his shield skills to become unstoppable. The anime features betrayal, redemption, and a unique class system—just like Jin-Woo’s journey.
Unique twist: Naofumi’s rapport system lets him bond with monsters, adding a fresh layer to power progression.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll, Netflix
Fan favorite: “The moment Naofumi unlocks his Wrath Shield is as satisfying as Jin-Woo’s first shadow army summon!” dexerto.com+1
B. Mushoku Tensei: Jobless Reincarnation

Why it’s similar: Rudeus starts as a weak child but grinds his magic and swordsmanship to godlike levels. The magic system is deep, and his growth is earned through hard work—just like Jin-Woo’s training.
Unique twist: Reincarnation and lifelong skill mastery make his power feel realistic.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll
Fan favorite: “Rudeus’ Water God Style swordplay is as iconic as Jin-Woo’s Shadow Extraction!” theflagshipeclipse.com+1
C. Arifureta: From Commonplace to World’s Strongest

Why it’s similar: Hajime Nagumo is betrayed and left for dead, but he unlocks OP transmutation magic and builds a harem of powerful allies. His ruthless, strategic fights mirror Jin-Woo’s tactical battles.
Unique twist: Hajime’s custom weapons and alchemy set him apart from typical isekai heroes.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll, Funimation
Fan favorite: “Hajime’s Orichalcum armor and Euhrydale are as cool as Jin-Woo’s Demon King’s Castle!” theflagshipeclipse.com+1
D. The Misfit of Demon King Academy

Why it’s similar: Anos Voldigoad is a reincarnated demon king who’s OP from the start but still faces challenges. His magic and demonic powers are as overwhelming as Jin-Woo’s shadow army.
Unique twist: Anos rebuilds his demon kingdom while dealing with political intrigue.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll
Fan favorite: “Anos’ Gigant Impact spell is the kind of over-the-top power fans love!” theflagshipeclipse.com
E. Berserk of Gluttony

Why it’s similar: Fate starts weak but gains the Gluttony skill, letting him devour enemies to level up—just like Jin-Woo’s system. The dark, brutal world and power progression are major draws.
Unique twist: The skill system is tied to sin and sacrifice, adding moral complexity.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll
Fan favorite: “Fate’s Gluttony evolution is as addictive as Jin-Woo’s System messages!” cbr.com
F. Shangri-La Frontier

Why it’s similar: Sunraku traps himself in a VR game and grinds to become the strongest player. The game mechanics and strategic battles feel like Solo Leveling’s dungeon raids.
Unique twist: Sunraku breaks the game’s meta, making every fight creative.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll
Fan favorite: “Sunraku’s ‘I’ll solo this boss’ energy is pure Jin-Woo vibes!” gamerant.com

2. Dark Fantasy Anime with Action & Leveling (2025 Picks)
For fans who love Solo Leveling’s dark tone, high stakes, and brutal battles, these 2025 dark fantasy anime are must-watches:
A. Gachiakuta

Why it’s similar: Set in a dystopian floating city, Rudo fights monsters and uncovers conspiracies—just like Jin-Woo’s hunter world. The action is visceral, and the power system is unique.
Unique twist: Graffiti-inspired visuals and urban rebellion themes make it stand out.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll (Summer 2025)
Fan favorite: “Gachiakuta’s monster designs are as terrifying as Solo Leveling’s Ant Monarch!” geekycritic.com+1
B. Tougen Anki

Why it’s similar: Shiki discovers his Oni powers and battles rival clans in a blood-soaked world. The power progression and dark atmosphere are reminiscent of Solo Leveling’s later arcs.
Unique twist: Oni transformation mechanics and clan politics add depth.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll (July 2025)
Fan favorite: “Shiki’s Oni form is as intimidating as Jin-Woo’s Demon King mode!” ranker.com
C. Chainsaw Man

Why it’s similar: Denji starts as a nobody but gains devil powers and fights for survival. The brutal action and power-ups are Solo Leveling levels of intense.
Unique twist: Denji’s Chainsaw Devil form is as iconic as Jin-Woo’s Shadow Monarch.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll
Fan favorite: “Denji’s ‘I just want a normal life’ struggle hits as hard as Jin-Woo’s early hunter days!” dexerto.com+1
D. Jujutsu Kaisen

Why it’s similar: Yuji Itadori swallows a cursed object and gains OP powers, but must train and level up to survive. The power system is well-defined, and the battles are visceral.
Unique twist: Cursed energy mechanics and team dynamics set it apart.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll, HBO Max
Fan favorite: “Yuji’s Divergent Fist is as hype as Jin-Woo’s Ashborn powers!” dexerto.com

3. Unique Power Progression Systems (Like Solo Leveling’s System)
These anime feature innovative power systems that make progression addictive and rewarding:
A. Log Horizon

Why it’s similar: Players are trapped in a game world and must level up classes and skills to survive. The strategy and world-building are top-tier.
Unique twist: Guild politics and economy play a huge role.
Where to watch: Hulu, Crunchyroll gamerant.com+1
B. DanMachi (Is It Wrong to Try to Pick Up Girls in a Dungeon?)

Why it’s similar: Bell Cranel starts as a weak adventurer but levels up through dungeon crawling. The RPG mechanics and goddess blessings are a fun twist.
Unique twist: Familia (guild) system and romantic subplots.
Where to watch: HIDIVE, Crunchyroll anime-planet.com
C. The Unwanted Undead Adventurer

Why it’s similar: Rentt Faina becomes an undead but retains his adventurer skills, forcing him to adapt and level up in a cruel world.
Unique twist: Undead mechanics and survival horror elements.
Where to watch: Crunchyroll ranker.com
